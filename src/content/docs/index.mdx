Use these chapters to move from interest to a clear solar decision. Start with your goal, collect the facts that shape each quote, then decide whether solar, batteries, financing, and a specific installer fit your home.

## The Homeowner Solar Path

<div class="journey-map">
  <div class="journey-step">
    <span class="step-number">1</span>
    <div>
      <strong>Define the job solar needs to do.</strong>
      <p>Decide whether the priority is lower bills, outage backup, cleaner energy, predictable costs, or preparing for future loads like an EV or heat pump.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="journey-step">
    <span class="step-number">2</span>
    <div>
      <strong>Check whether the home is a reasonable candidate.</strong>
      <p>Check roof age, shade, roof space, main panel location, approvals, and work needed before installation.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="journey-step">
    <span class="step-number">3</span>
    <div>
      <strong>Use your bill before you use a quote.</strong>
      <p>Find monthly kWh usage, seasonal patterns, rate-plan clues, fixed charges, and planned household changes.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="journey-step">
    <span class="step-number">4</span>
    <div>
      <strong>Compare designs, not sales claims.</strong>
      <p>Ask for system size, equipment, roof layout, production estimate, utility rules, incentives, payment terms, and exclusions.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="journey-step">
    <span class="step-number">5</span>
    <div>
      <strong>Decide with a clean yes, no, or not-yet answer.</strong>
      <p>The right answer could be sign, wait, skip batteries, get another quote, or walk away.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## What To Collect Before Quotes

<div class="homeowner-checklist">
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> One recent electric bill, plus 12 months of usage if your utility account provides it.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Photos of the main electric panel, utility meter, roof surfaces, attic access, and visible shade sources.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Roof age, known leaks, planned roof work, and any HOA, landlord, or co-owner approval requirements.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Your main goal: bill reduction, backup, carbon reduction, budget predictability, or future electric loads.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Questions you want every installer to answer in writing, not only during a sales call.</label>
</div>

## A Simple Decision Rule

<div class="field-note">
  <strong>Do not start by asking, "How much will solar save me?"</strong>
  <p>Ask, "What must be true for solar to make sense here?" The answer depends on roof condition, usage, utility rules, quote math, financing, and estimate risk.</p>
</div>
