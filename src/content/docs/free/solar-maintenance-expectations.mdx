## What You Will Learn

- Know what routine solar ownership usually involves.
- Understand monitoring signals that deserve follow-up.
- Keep warranty and service responsibilities clear.

## Key Ideas

### Monitoring Helps Catch Problems Early

Production apps can show whether the system is online and producing. Weather and seasons matter, but sudden unexplained drops should be investigated.

### Cleaning Needs Vary by Site

Rain, dust, pollen, wildfire smoke, trees, roof pitch, and local conditions influence whether cleaning is useful and how often it should be considered.

### Warranty Coverage Has Layers

Panel, inverter, battery, monitoring, roof penetration, and workmanship warranties may come from different parties with different claim processes.

## Practical Steps

1. Ask how you will monitor production after installation.
2. Confirm who handles warranty questions for panels, inverters, batteries, and workmanship.
3. Keep installer contact details and system documents in one place.

## Questions To Ask

- Which monitoring app will I use, and who helps if it stops reporting?
- What workmanship warranty applies, and what exactly does it cover?
- Who should I contact first for production, roof, or equipment concerns?

## Watch-Outs

- Assuming all warranty claims go through one company.
- Mistaking normal seasonal variation for a system fault without checking context.
- Losing final plans, permits, inspection records, and equipment serial details.
