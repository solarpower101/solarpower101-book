## What You Will Learn

- Know what routine solar ownership involves.
- Understand monitoring signals that deserve follow-up.
- Keep warranty and service responsibilities clear.
- Know what documents and contacts to keep after installation.

## Real-World Scenarios

<div class="field-note">
  <strong>Scenario 1: production drops suddenly after months of normal output.</strong>
  <p>Cloudy weather and seasonal changes are normal. A sudden drop, offline inverter, missing monitoring data, or repeated error needs follow-up. Take screenshots and contact support.</p>
</div>

<div class="field-note">
  <strong>Scenario 2: the system looks dirty after pollen season.</strong>
  <p>Cleaning can help in some locations, but roof pitch, rain, safety, water quality, and dust matter. Check whether production loss justifies professional cleaning.</p>
</div>

<div class="field-note">
  <strong>Scenario 3: a roof leak appears two years after installation.</strong>
  <p>The homeowner needs installation records, roof-penetration warranty terms, installer contact details, and photos. Warranty responsibility may differ between roof materials, mounting workmanship, and unrelated roof aging.</p>
</div>

## Key Ideas

### Monitoring Helps Catch Problems Early

Production apps show whether the system is online and producing. Weather and seasons matter, but sudden unexplained drops need review.

### Cleaning Needs Vary by Site

Rain, dust, pollen, wildfire smoke, trees, roof pitch, and local conditions affect cleaning needs.

### Warranty Coverage Has Layers

Panel, inverter, battery, monitoring, roof penetration, and workmanship warranties may come from different parties with different claim processes.

## Practical Steps

1. Ask how you will monitor production after installation.
2. Confirm who handles warranty questions for panels, inverters, batteries, and workmanship.
3. Keep installer contact details and system documents in one place.
4. Save final plans, permit records, inspection approvals, PTO notice, equipment model numbers, and monitoring login details.
5. Take screenshots of normal production after the system has been operating so later changes have context.

## Questions To Ask

- Which monitoring app will I use, and who helps if it stops reporting?
- What workmanship warranty applies, and what exactly does it cover?
- Who should I contact first for production, roof, or equipment concerns?
- What production change should trigger a service request?
- What maintenance could void a warranty if performed incorrectly?
- If the installer goes out of business, which manufacturer warranties still apply and how are claims filed?

## What Good Looks Like

<div class="homeowner-checklist">
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> The homeowner has monitoring access and understands basic normal-vs-abnormal signals.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Final documents, approvals, and equipment details are stored in one place.</label>
  <label><input type="checkbox" /> Warranty terms separate workmanship, roof penetrations, panels, inverters, batteries, and monitoring.</label>

## Watch-Outs

- Assuming all warranty claims go through one company.
- Mistaking normal seasonal variation for a system fault without checking context.
- Losing final plans, permits, inspection records, and equipment serial details.
- Climbing on the roof for cleaning or inspection without proper safety equipment.
- Waiting months to report an offline inverter or monitoring failure.
