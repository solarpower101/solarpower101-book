## What You Will Learn

- Compare installers using the same criteria.
- Read quote assumptions instead of focusing only on headline price.
- Identify contract and communication details before signing.

## Key Ideas

### A Quote Is a Design Proposal, Not Just a Price

System size, equipment, production estimate, roof layout, electrical work, financing, warranty terms, and schedule all shape the value of the proposal.

### Installer Accountability Should Be Clear

Homeowners should know who sells the project, who designs it, who installs it, who handles permits, and who provides post-install support.

### Contract Terms Matter Before Work Starts

Review payment milestones, cancellation terms, change orders, lien releases, equipment substitutions, and production estimate language before signing.

## Practical Steps

1. Ask each installer to explain system size, estimated production, and assumptions in plain language.
2. Compare warranties, payment terms, cancellation terms, and who performs the installation.
3. Avoid treating estimated savings as a guarantee.

## Questions To Ask

- Who will perform the installation: employees, subcontractors, or a partner crew?
- What assumptions drive the production and savings estimate?
- What contract terms apply if equipment, design, or timeline changes?

## Watch-Outs

- Choosing only by lowest monthly payment or highest claimed savings.
- Signing before the roof, panel, and utility requirements are understood.
- Accepting verbal promises that do not appear in the proposal or contract.
