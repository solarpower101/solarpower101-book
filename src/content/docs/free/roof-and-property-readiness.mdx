## What You Will Learn

- Know which property details affect system design.
- Spot roof or electrical issues that may change scope.
- Prepare useful photos before a site review.

## Key Ideas

### Roof Condition Matters Before Solar Design

If a roof is near replacement age, it is often better to resolve that before installing equipment that may need to be removed for later roof work.

### Shade Changes Production Throughout the Year

Trees, chimneys, vents, and neighboring structures can reduce output at different times of day and in different seasons.

### Electrical Readiness Can Affect Cost

Main panel capacity, meter location, conduit runs, and local code requirements can change the installation scope beyond the panels themselves.

## Practical Steps

1. Estimate your roof age and note any repairs planned in the next few years.
2. Look for shade from trees, chimneys, neighboring buildings, or vents during the day.
3. Confirm who can approve changes to the property before scheduling a site review.

## Questions To Ask

- Does my roof need repair or replacement before installation?
- Will vents, skylights, dormers, or trees limit the array layout?
- Do you expect a main panel upgrade or other electrical work?

## Watch-Outs

- Skipping roof condition questions because the solar quote looks attractive.
- Using only a satellite image when shade or roof complexity needs closer review.
- Forgetting HOA, landlord, or co-owner approval requirements.

## Next Action

Take clear photos of the roof area, main panel, and visible shading.
