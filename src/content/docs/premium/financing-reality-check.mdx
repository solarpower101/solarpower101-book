## Homeowner Scenario

You are deciding whether the financing structure helps you buy solar responsibly or hides costs behind a comfortable monthly payment.

## Required Inputs

- Cash price.
- Financed principal.
- Dealer fees or financed-cost difference.
- APR, term, monthly payment, and payment escalators.
- Lease or PPA rate, escalator, buyout terms, and transfer terms.
- Incentive assumptions and responsibility for claiming them.

## Decision Process

1. Compare the cash price with the financed principal.
2. Calculate total payments over the full term.
3. Identify dealer fees, escalators, prepayment rules, and buyout language.
4. Separate utility-bill savings estimates from financing obligations.
5. Decide whether the structure fits your risk tolerance and ownership goals.

## Example Comparison

A $25,000 cash system financed at $34,000 with a low promotional APR may cost more than a higher-APR loan with no dealer fee. The APR is not enough; compare total cost and contract flexibility.

## Red Flags

- The quote shows only monthly payment and estimated bill savings.
- Dealer fee or financed principal is not disclosed.
- A lease or PPA escalator is treated as minor without showing long-term effect.
- Tax credit assumptions are used to make a required re-amortization look automatic.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What is the cash price and what is the financed price?
- Are there dealer fees, prepayment penalties, or required tax credit payments?
- What happens if I sell the home?
- Who owns the equipment under this structure?

## Decision Output

Create a financing comparison with total payments, effective cost warnings, key contract questions, and a plain recommendation to continue, renegotiate, or pause.
