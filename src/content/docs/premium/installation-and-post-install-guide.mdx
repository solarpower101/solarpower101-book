## Homeowner Scenario

You signed a solar contract and need to know what should happen next, what documents to keep, and when to ask for help.

## Required Inputs

- Signed contract and final design.
- Permit status, installation date, inspection notes, and utility application status.
- Permission to operate notice.
- Monitoring app access.
- Warranty documents, equipment manuals, and installer contact information.

## Decision Process

1. Track each project milestone from design approval through permission to operate.
2. Keep signed documents, permits, inspections, and warranty files in one folder.
3. Confirm monitoring access after activation.
4. Compare early production expectations against season, weather, and system status.
5. Identify when an installer support request is appropriate.

## Example Tracker

If installation is complete but the utility has not granted permission to operate, the system may be off or limited. The next action is to confirm interconnection status instead of assuming the panels are failing.

## Red Flags

- The installer cannot explain permit, inspection, or utility status.
- You do not receive monitoring access after activation.
- The system is operating but warranty and equipment documents are missing.
- Production concerns are dismissed without checking system alerts.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What milestone are we waiting for now?
- Who submits interconnection documents to the utility?
- When should I receive monitoring access?
- What documents should I keep for warranty, insurance, and resale?

## Decision Output

Create an installation tracker with milestone status, missing documents, monitoring expectations, support triggers, and next action.

Use an installation and post-install checklist covering permits, inspection, permission to operate, monitoring, warranty documents, and support contacts.
