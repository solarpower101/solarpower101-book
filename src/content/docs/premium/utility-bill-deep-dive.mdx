## Homeowner Scenario

You have a recent electric bill and at least one installer has asked about usage, savings, or rate plans.

## Required Inputs

- Monthly kWh usage from the current bill.
- Any available 12-month usage history.
- Rate plan name, fixed charges, delivery charges, and time-of-use periods.
- Net metering, export credit, or solar billing language if shown.
- Notes about upcoming usage changes such as EV charging, heat pumps, or household size.

## Decision Process

1. Record the current month kWh and bill total separately.
2. Add monthly usage into an annual usage estimate when 12 months are available.
3. Identify fixed charges that solar may not offset.
4. Mark time-of-use or export language that affects savings assumptions.
5. Compare installer production and savings assumptions against the bill data.

## Example Calculation

If the bill history shows 9,600 kWh per year and a proposal assumes 12,400 kWh of usage, the installer should explain the difference. A future EV may justify higher usage, but the assumption should be explicit.

## Red Flags

- Savings are presented without annual usage.
- Fixed charges are treated as avoidable energy charges.
- The quote ignores time-of-use periods even though the bill names them.
- Export credit assumptions are not documented.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What annual usage number did you use?
- Which utility rate plan did you model?
- How did you treat fixed charges and export credits?
- Does the model assume any future home electrification or EV charging?

## Decision Output

Create a bill input summary with annual usage, seasonal pattern, fixed-charge notes, rate-plan questions, and quote assumptions that require verification.
