## Homeowner Scenario

You have one solar proposal and need to know whether it is complete enough to trust, compare, or challenge before signing.

## Required Inputs

- System size in kW DC.
- Panel count and panel model.
- Inverter, optimizer, microinverter, or battery details.
- Gross cost, incentives, net cost, and financing terms.
- Estimated annual production.
- Warranty terms and installer assumptions.

## Decision Process

1. Calculate gross cost per watt by dividing gross project cost by system watts.
2. Separate incentives from the contract price so the project cost is visible.
3. Check whether equipment models and warranties are specific.
4. Compare estimated production against roof, shade, and usage assumptions.
5. List every missing detail that would block a fair comparison.

## Example Calculation

A $28,800 gross quote for an 8.0 kW system equals $3.60 per watt before incentives. If another quote only shows a monthly payment, ask for the gross cash price and financing terms before comparing.

## Red Flags

- Monthly payment is emphasized while cash price is hidden.
- Equipment is described generically as "premium panels" or "tier 1."
- Production estimates lack assumptions about shade, azimuth, or utility rules.
- Incentives are shown as guaranteed personal savings.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What is the gross cash price before incentives and financing?
- Which exact panel, inverter, and battery models are included?
- What production model and shade assumptions did you use?
- What warranty is from the manufacturer and what warranty is from your company?

## Decision Output

Create a quote confidence rating with cost per watt, missing details, red flags, assumption checks, and follow-up questions.

## Premium Artifact

Use a quote comparison worksheet with cost per watt, equipment fields, production assumptions, financing notes, warranty terms, and confidence rating.
