## Homeowner Scenario

You are close to signing and need one structured review before the decision becomes harder to unwind.

## Required Inputs

- Contract, proposal, and any financing agreement.
- Cancellation window and notice method.
- Scope of work, equipment list, and payment schedule.
- Warranty terms, roof penetration language, and production guarantee language.
- Permitting, interconnection, inspection, and utility responsibility.
- Lien, arbitration, assignment, or transfer language if present.

## Decision Process

1. Confirm the contract matches the quote you intended to accept.
2. Identify cancellation deadline, method, and required recipient.
3. Check whether equipment, scope, warranties, and payments are specific.
4. Mark unclear production, roof, lien, and change-order language.
5. Decide whether to sign, request revisions, or pause.

## Example Review

A contract includes the correct system size but says equipment may be substituted with equivalent products. The output should require written substitution standards before signing, especially if warranty or inverter architecture affects the decision.

## Red Flags

- Cancellation terms are hard to find.
- Equipment may be substituted without written approval.
- Payment milestones are due before clear project progress.
- Production guarantee language is vague or weaker than the sales presentation.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- Where is my cancellation right stated and how do I use it?
- What equipment can be substituted and who approves it?
- What happens if permitting, roof work, or interconnection changes the scope?
- Who is responsible for roof leaks related to installation penetrations?

## Decision Output

Create a pre-sign report with red flags, missing clauses, required clarifications, and a sign / revise / pause recommendation.

## Premium Artifact

Use a contract red flag checklist with cancellation, equipment, scope, warranty, roof, lien, payment, and production-guarantee checks.
