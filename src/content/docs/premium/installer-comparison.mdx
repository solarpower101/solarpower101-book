## Homeowner Scenario

You have multiple proposals and need a fair comparison that goes beyond the lowest monthly payment.

## Required Inputs

- Two to four quotes.
- Installer license, credential, insurance, and subcontracting details.
- Equipment, system size, production estimate, and battery details.
- Cash price, financing price, cancellation terms, and payment schedule.
- Workmanship warranty, roof penetration language, and communication notes.

## Decision Process

1. Normalize each quote to gross cash price, system size, and cost per watt.
2. Compare equipment and warranty coverage side by side.
3. Separate production assumptions from actual guarantees.
4. Score contract clarity, cancellation terms, and installer responsiveness.
5. Identify hidden differences before selecting a finalist.

## Example Comparison

Quote A is cheaper but uses a smaller system and excludes a likely panel upgrade. Quote B costs more but includes defined electrical scope and a clearer workmanship warranty. The comparison should flag the missing scope before declaring either quote better.

## Red Flags

- One quote omits work that another quote explicitly includes.
- The lowest monthly payment depends on a large dealer fee or tax credit assumption.
- Subcontracting, workmanship warranty, or roof responsibility is unclear.
- The installer resists giving a cash price.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What scope is excluded and could become a change order?
- Who performs the installation and who services workmanship issues?
- What happens if production or interconnection assumptions change?
- What are the cancellation rights and payment milestones?

## Decision Output

Create an installer scorecard with side-by-side quote data, hidden differences, red flags, and recommended follow-up questions.

## Premium Artifact

Use an installer scorecard that scores price clarity, scope completeness, equipment specificity, warranty strength, contract risk, and communication quality.
