## Homeowner Scenario

You are interested in solar but do not know whether requesting quotes now will create useful proposals or rushed sales conversations.

## Required Inputs

- Roof age, material, and any known repair history.
- Shade notes from trees, chimneys, vents, dormers, and neighboring buildings.
- Photos of the roof planes, main electrical panel, meter, and likely equipment areas.
- Ownership, HOA, landlord, co-owner, or permitting constraints.
- Recent electric bill and rough project timeline.

## Decision Process

1. Score roof condition as ready, needs contractor review, or likely delay.
2. Mark shade and layout constraints that could reduce useful roof area.
3. Check whether electrical equipment looks modern enough for installer review.
4. Confirm who must approve the project before a contract can move forward.
5. Decide whether the next step is quote requests, roof or electrical work, or more document collection.

## Example Assessment

A homeowner has a 19-year-old asphalt shingle roof, moderate afternoon tree shade, a 125 amp panel, and no HOA. The readiness output should not be "get three quotes immediately." A better output is: request a roof condition opinion, ask installers whether panel work is likely, and avoid signing until roof timing is clear.

## Red Flags

- Roof replacement is likely within the next few years.
- The installer avoids discussing shade or roof age.
- The proposal assumes easy interconnection before anyone reviews the panel and meter.
- A required approver has not been identified.

## Questions To Ask Installer

- What roof condition would make you delay installation?
- Do you expect a main panel upgrade, service work, or additional electrical scope?
- What photos or documents do you need before designing the first proposal?
- How will shade be measured before final design?

## Decision Output

Produce a readiness score of ready, needs review, or delay recommended. Include the blockers, the documents to collect, and the conditions that must be resolved before signing.

## Premium Artifact

Use a roof readiness worksheet with fields for roof age, shade, electrical notes, approvals, blockers, quote readiness, and the next action.
